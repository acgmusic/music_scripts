Notes

All music by Falcom Sound Team jdk (Hayato Sonoda, Takahiro Unisuga, Saki Momiyama, Tomokatsu Hagiuda), with outsourced work by Yukihiro Jindo, Toshiharu Okajima and Noriyuki Kamikura.

Composer breakdown leaked by an anonymous user with ties to Falcom: https://www65.atwiki.jp/falcom_staff/pages/141.html

Composition (external):
Yukihiro Jindo (Disc 1: 1, 16 / Disc 2: 5, 12, 15, 17)
Toshiharu Okajima (Disc 1: 12, 13 / Disc 2: 8, 9)
Noriyuki Kamikura (Disc 1: 15 / Disc 2: 1, 14)

Arrangement (internal):
Hayato Sonoda (Disc 1: 4, 6, 7, 8, 9, 11, 14, 19 / Disc 2: 3, 6, 7, 10, 11)
Takahiro Unisuga (Disc 1: 2, 3, 10, 17 / Disc 2: 4, 13)
Saki Momiyama (Disc 1: 5, 18 / Disc 2: 16)
Tomokatsu Hagiuda (Disc 2: 2)

Notes:
• Arrangement for the Ys IV tracks was handled by Sound Team jdk (internal), whereas composition for all new tracks was outsourced to the contractors (external).
• The 22 tracks from Ys IV were originally composed by the Falcom Sound Team J.D.K. of 1992/3 (Atsushi Shirakawa, Naoki Kaneda, Masaru Nakajima)
• D2-7 is a new composition for Ys Eternal (1997), its real composer remains still unknown.
